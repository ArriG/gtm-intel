# UK Dental Practices — Sector Pack

For sellers targeting UK dental practices: independents, small groups, and DSOs.

## Sources (priority order — max 7)

Search across these sources only. Stop each search after one attempt if nothing useful returns.

### 1. Website + Google Business Profile + recent reviews

Search the practice website. Then search `[practice name] [town] site:google.com/maps` for the Google Business listing. Look for:

- Star rating + review count (proxy for practice health)
- Recent review themes — wait times, booking, communication = operational pain
- Services advertised — Invisalign, implants, cosmetic = private-mix focus
- Last update / photo freshness (proxy for principal engagement)

Cite review **themes**, never individual reviewer names. Don't quote reviews older than 6 months.

### 2. CQC inspection report

Search `site:cqc.org.uk [practice name]`. Look for:

- Latest inspection rating across all five domains (Safe, Effective, Caring, Responsive, Well-led)
- Any "Requires improvement" or "Inadequate" rating
- Specific breaches (e.g. "breach of regulation 12 — safe care and treatment")
- Date of last inspection

A recent inspection with anything other than "Good" across all domains is a major buying signal. **CQC covers England only** — for Scotland use Care Inspectorate; for Wales use HIW.

### 3. Companies House

Search `site:find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk [practice name]`. Look for:

- Recent director appointments or resignations (new principal = buying window)
- Recently filed accounts (full accounts reveal revenue trajectory; abbreviated accounts are thin)
- Multiple practice entities under same control (multi-site signal)

### 4. GDC register

Search `site:gdc-uk.org [principal name]`. Confirms principal and associates are registered. Flag any fitness-to-practise history (rare but high-signal).

### 5. UK dental jobs

Search `[practice name] jobs site:bdjjobs.com OR site:indeed.co.uk` with dental filter. Apply this role → pain mapping:

| Role hiring | Most likely pain |
|-------------|------------------|
| Associate dentist        | Capacity issue OR associate retention problem |
| Practice manager         | Principal trying to step back from day-to-day ops |
| Dental nurse / hygienist | UK-wide workforce shortage |
| Treatment coordinator    | Private-mix growth, conversion-minded principal |

### 6. LinkedIn — principal / practice manager

Search `site:linkedin.com [principal name] [practice name]` and `site:linkedin.com [practice manager name] [practice name]`. Owner-operators post less than enterprise CXOs but when they do, it's high signal. Capture exact quotes.

### 7. Dental trade press

Search `[practice name] site:dentistry.co.uk OR site:bdj.co.uk OR site:the-probe.co.uk OR site:dentaltribune.co.uk` for coverage in the last 12 months — awards, expansions, group moves, controversy.

## Why-now triggers to hunt for

When found, the call is justified this week:

- CQC rating change in last 12 months (especially downgrade)
- New director or principal at Companies House in last 6 months
- Practice acquisition or multi-site expansion
- Hiring 2+ associates simultaneously (growth OR turnover)
- Hiring a practice manager (operational pain admission)
- Local NHS contract changes affecting their region
- Principal posting publicly about NHS-vs-private mix struggles

## Buyer language

Use the sector's words, not generic SaaS:

- They say **"list size"**, not "number of patients"
- They say **"UDA"**, not "treatment volume"
- They say **"private mix"**, not "revenue split"
- They say **"principal"**, not "owner" or "CEO"
- They say **"associates"**, not "staff" or "employees"
- They say **"chairside time"**, not "appointment duration"

Avoid "patients" when discussing the business (it's "the list"). Avoid "users" when discussing the team (it's "the practice").

## Buying committee shape

Match committee size to company shape — over-staffing the committee feels generic.

| Company shape | Typical committee |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Single-site practice | 1 buyer — the principal (sometimes practice manager influences) |
| Small group (2–5 sites) | Principal + operations director + sometimes finance |
| DSO / corporate (Mydentist, Portman) | Clinical director + COO + CFO + regional ops + IT |

## Manual research tips (surface in brief)

We can't reliably reach these. Tell the AE to spend 10 minutes here before the call:

- **GDPUK forum** — principals discuss real problems candidly (login required)
- **Dentist Owners UK** Facebook group — live owner-operator pain (private group)
- **Practice Plan Insiders** Facebook group — practice management focus
- **Confidence in Dentistry** podcast — what principals are listening to
- The practice's own **YouTube / Instagram** for tone-of-voice

## Avoidance rules

- **Don't lead with "AI-powered"** — UK dental is sceptical of tech hype
- **Don't quote reviewers by name** — patient privacy norms are strong
- **Don't assume cloud-native** — much of UK dental is still on-prem (SOE, R4, EXACT)
- **Don't conflate NHS-heavy and private practices** — opposite pain patterns
- **Don't treat single-site and DSO targets the same** — DSOs need CH + group press; single-site practices need CQC + reviews
