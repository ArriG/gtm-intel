# UK Financial Services — Sector Pack

For sellers targeting UK banks, insurers, reinsurers, and regulated financial institutions.

## Sources (priority order — max 7)

Search across these sources only. Stop each search after one attempt if nothing useful returns.

### 1. Company website + investor relations

Read homepage, about, and product pages. Search `[company name] investor relations OR annual report` for strategic priorities, transformation programmes, and stated risks.

### 2. Companies House

Search `site:find-and-update.company-information.service.gov.uk [company name]` for filings, directors, charges, and filing dates. Recent accounts filing delays can signal operational strain.

### 3. FCA register (when regulated)

Search `site:register.fca.org.uk [company name]` for authorisation status, permissions, and regulatory context. Useful for insurers, brokers, and fintechs.

### 4. UK job postings

Search `[company name] jobs site:linkedin.com/jobs OR site:indeed.co.uk OR site:efinancialcareers.com` — read descriptions for hiring priorities (underwriting ops, data, transformation, compliance). Job ads are high-signal for pain points.

### 5. LinkedIn leadership signals

Search `site:linkedin.com [CEO OR CFO OR CRO name] [company name]` for recent posts on transformation, risk, efficiency, or tech modernisation. Note exact quotes if found.

### 6. UK financial & insurance press

Search `[company name] site:ft.com OR site:insurancetimes.co.uk OR site:postonline.co.uk OR site:thebanker.com OR site:reinsurancene.ws` for coverage in the last 12 months — partnerships, leadership moves, results commentary.

### 7. Lloyd's / SFCR (when relevant)

For Lloyd's syndicates and insurers, search `[company name] SFCR OR site:lloyds.com` for solvency and underwriting performance signals.

## Role → pain mapping

| Role | Likely pain language |
|------|---------------------|
| Chief Underwriting Officer / Head of Underwriting | Manual workflows, slow quote turnaround, portfolio visibility |
| Chief Risk Officer | Model governance, data quality, regulatory reporting burden |
| CFO / Finance Director | Cost of legacy IT, transformation ROI, operational efficiency |
| CTO / CIO | Integration debt, cloud migration, vendor sprawl |
| Head of Operations | Straight-through processing, handoffs between teams, error rates |

## Buyer language

- They say **"bind"** and **"in-force"**, not "closed deals"
- They say **"underwriting appetite"**, not "ICP"
- They say **"bordereaux"** and **"claims leakage"** in insurance contexts
- Avoid generic SaaS jargon — lead with operational outcomes and regulatory awareness

## Manual research tips (surface in brief)

- **Insurance Insider / Post Magazine** paywalls — note the headline if visible, don't invent article text
- **Companies House filing PDFs** — skim strategic report section for transformation language
- **Glassdoor / eFinancialCareers reviews** — themes only, never quote employees by name

## Avoidance rules

- Don't conflate retail banking and Lloyd's market targets — different buying cycles
- Don't assume cloud-native — much of UK financial services is still on legacy core systems
- Don't invent FCA enforcement actions — verify on register or official press releases only
