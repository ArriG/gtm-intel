# AU Dental Practices — Sector Pack

For sellers targeting Australian dental practices: independents, small groups, and corporate dental groups.

## Sources (priority order — max 7)

Search across these sources only. Stop each search after one attempt if nothing useful returns.

### 1. Website + Google Business Profile + recent reviews

Search the practice website. Then search `[practice name] [suburb] site:google.com/maps` for the Google Business listing. Look for:

- Star rating + review count (proxy for practice health)
- Recent review themes — wait times, booking, communication = operational pain
- Services advertised — orthodontics, implants, cosmetic = private-mix focus
- Bulk billing flagged? CDBS / DVA acceptance? = patient mix signal
- Last update / photo freshness (proxy for principal engagement)

Cite review themes, never reviewer names. Date any review older than 6 months when quoting.

### 2. AHPRA / Dental Board of Australia register

Search `site:ahpra.gov.au [principal name]` or `site:dentalboard.gov.au [principal name]`. Confirms registration of principal and associates. Flag any current conditions, undertakings, or notations on registration — these signal regulatory pressure.

### 3. ASIC + ABN Lookup

Search `site:abr.business.gov.au [practice name]` for ABN, entity status, and entity type. For company entities, search `[practice name] ASIC` for director appointments or resignations and recent filings. New director = buying window. Multiple entities under same control = multi-site signal.

### 4. Seek + Indeed AU (dental filter)

Search `[practice name] jobs site:seek.com.au OR site:au.indeed.com`. Apply this role → pain mapping:

| Role hiring | Most likely pain |
|-------------|------------------|
| Associate dentist        | Capacity issue OR associate retention problem |
| Practice manager         | Principal trying to step back from day-to-day ops |
| Dental assistant / OHT   | Workforce shortage (acute in regional AU) |
| Treatment coordinator    | Private-mix growth, conversion-minded principal |

### 5. LinkedIn — principal / practice manager

Search `site:linkedin.com [principal name] [practice name]` and `site:linkedin.com [practice manager name] [practice name]`. Owner-operators post less than enterprise CXOs but when they do, it's high signal. Capture exact quotes.

### 6. Australian dental trade press

Search `[practice name] site:bitemagazine.com.au OR site:australasiandentalpractice.com OR site:ada.org.au` for coverage in the last 12 months — awards, expansions, group moves, controversy.

### 7. State health department notices (only when relevant)

For NSW: `site:health.nsw.gov.au`. For VIC: `site:health.vic.gov.au`. For QLD: `site:health.qld.gov.au`. Only worth searching when there's reason to expect a public notice — most practices won't have one.

## Why-now triggers to hunt for

When found, the call is justified this week:

- New director or principal at ASIC in last 6 months
- Practice acquisition or multi-site expansion (especially joining a group)
- Hiring 2+ associates simultaneously (growth OR turnover)
- Hiring a practice manager (operational pain admission)
- Bulk billing changes or CDBS eligibility shifts affecting their patient mix
- Health fund / insurer dispute publicly raised by the principal
- Corporate consolidation moves in their region (Pacific Smiles, Maven Dental, National Dental Care)

## Buyer language

Use the sector's words, not generic SaaS:

- They say **"patient base"** or **"active patients"**, not "users"
- They say **"private mix"**, not "revenue split"
- They say **"principal"**, not "owner" or "CEO"
- They say **"associates"**, not "staff"
- They say **"DA"** (dental assistant), **"OHT"** (oral health therapist), **"hygienist"**
- They say **"chairs"** (operatory count = capacity), **"chair time"**
- They say **"health fund"**, not "insurance company"
- They say **"bulk bill"**, **"CDBS"** (Child Dental Benefits Schedule), **"DVA"**

## Buying committee shape

Match committee size to company shape — over-staffing the committee feels generic.

| Company shape | Typical committee |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Single-site practice | 1 buyer — the principal (sometimes practice manager influences) |
| Small group (2–5 sites) | Principal + operations director + sometimes finance |
| Corporate group (Pacific Smiles, Maven, NDC) | Clinical director + COO + CFO + state/regional ops + IT |

## Manual research tips (surface in brief)

We can't reliably reach these. Tell the AE to spend 10 minutes here before the call:

- **ADA state branch** news pages (e.g. ADAVB for Victoria) — what local principals are reading
- **Bite Magazine** opinion and podcast pieces — tone-of-voice for AU principals
- Practice-owner Facebook groups — owner-operator pain (private groups)
- The practice's own **YouTube / Instagram** for tone-of-voice

## Avoidance rules

- **Don't lead with "AI-powered"** — AU dental is sceptical of tech hype
- **Don't quote reviewers by name** — patient privacy norms are strong
- **Don't assume cloud-native** — much of AU dental is still on-prem (D4W, Praktika, Exact, Dental4Windows)
- **Don't conflate bulk-billing-heavy and private practices** — opposite pain patterns
- **Don't treat single-site and corporate dental targets the same** — corporates need ASIC + group press; single-site practices need reviews + AHPRA + ASIC
- **Don't reference "Medicare for dental"** as an imminent change — politically sensitive, inconsistent across briefs
